# Folio launch email (draft v2 — in J.D. Siler voice)

Rewritten after seeing the previous "READ THE FIRST CHAPTER FREE" send. Continues softly
from that email's tease about "there's a new book coming," acknowledges the reader
without making them do work, and pivots to Folio as the *place* where all of it lives.
The Kickstarter announcement is deliberately held for a later email once pre-launch is live.

---

## Subject line options

1. `The reason I went quiet`
2. `About that thing I've been building — you're first in`
3. `Where the new book will live (and 50 spots on the house)`

I'd lean **#1** — it lands as a direct continuation of the last email's confessional
tone. "The reason I went quiet" almost re-opens the previous conversation. High open rate.

## Preheader

`The place where Embers lives — and where the next book will too.`

---

## Body

Hey,

Quick continuation of the last note — that new book I mentioned is still coming, and I'm
still much closer to the end of that road than the start. Progress this month has been
good. More on it soon.

But there's something else I've been building alongside it, and I want to open it to you
today.

Its name is **Folio**. It's the place where *Embers of Elekra* has been quietly living
this whole time — the reason that first chapter opens instantly in your browser with no
account, no signup, no gatekeeper. It's a publishing platform I've been writing for
writers like us, and it's live now at **[onfolio.press](https://www.onfolio.press)**.

The short version of what it does:

- Your imprint page, your imprint. Custom accent color, hero image, header font — the whole page reads like *you*, not like a marketplace.
- Publish free, paid, or private — your call, per book.
- 0% commission on tips. 0% commission on Press subscriptions to your work. Featured Boost placement when you want the extra visibility, no ad-selling model when you don't.
- Social media product photos generated from your covers (Instagram, Twitter, Pinterest — real mockups, not floating rectangles).
- A public Shelf where readers browse and find you.

**Here's what I'm doing for the mailing list**

The first 50 of you get:

- **12 months free on Folio Press Imprint** — the top tier, everything unlocked. Normally $12/month.
- **A "✨ Founding contributor" chip** on your imprint page, dated the month you claim it. When a reader browses the Shelf a year from now and sees your name, they'll know you were here at the start.
- **$19 Featured Boost credit** on your first release once it clears the Shelf — a week of top-of-shelf placement with the gold-border treatment.
- **A direct line to me** — replies to this email land in my inbox. Something's broken, something's missing, I want to hear it.

The ask, if you're in:

1. Post at least one thing you've already got finished. A poem, a chapter, a short
   story, an old novel that never went anywhere. Doesn't have to be new. It doesn't have
   to be perfect.
2. Once you've clicked around, leave one honest review in the feedback box. Good or bad,
   both help.
3. If you enjoy it, tell one or two writer friends who might too.

That's it. No schedule, no promotion required, no commitments. You can post one poem
and disappear for a year — you'll still be a founding contributor when you come back.

**How to claim**

Reply to this email with just **"in"** and I'll set you up. Takes me about a minute per
person, and I'll send back the URL of your new imprint page once it's live.

If you'd rather poke around first, head to [onfolio.press](https://www.onfolio.press),
click *Write*, and publish something. Come back to this email when you're ready and I'll
add the Founding chip.

I've missed being in your inbox. Let's pick this back up.

— J.D. Siler

## Style notes for pasting into MailerLite

- Reuse the same template as the last email (cream bg, dark accent button). If MailerLite has "Duplicate campaign" on the last one, start there.
- Pull-quote header option: `"The reason I went quiet is that I was building somewhere for us to write."` — set in the same big bold serif as the "skies of Stella" line. Optional; can also start with the "Hey," if you want it plainer.
- Primary CTA button below the offer list: `CLAIM MY FOUNDING SPOT →` with a `mailto:folio@jacobsiler.com?subject=in&body=in` link so replies come pre-filled.
- Secondary CTA at the end of the body: `Poke around Folio first →` linking to `https://www.onfolio.press`.
- Keep footer + unsubscribe exactly as last time (same footer text, same social icon).
- Send window: same time-of-day as the last email (whenever that landed). Consistency helps.
- Segment: writers/authors only if you have that split. If not, send to all — the ask is soft enough that readers who ignore it aren't burned.

## What NOT to include (for this send)

- **No mention of the Kickstarter beyond the vague "new book" continuation.** Save the Kickstarter for its own email once pre-launch is live and the "watch this" button exists. Muddying the two dilutes both.
- **No feature list beyond what's above.** The bullets are already a lot; the "here's every product photo template" details go on the /press page they click through to.
- **No pricing detail beyond the $12/mo offset.** The offer is 12 months free — don't spend column inches justifying what they'd pay after.

## When the Kickstarter is ready

Follow-up email lands whenever pre-launch is live. It becomes the natural sequel:

> "The book I've been quiet about is up on Kickstarter now. Pre-launch is live — you can
> follow it here for launch-day notification. Founding contributors, your Folio comp
> includes early-bird access to the pledge form 24 hours before public launch."

That last line is a bonus perk we can add — early access to the pledge form for founding contributors. Costs nothing, gives the launch email a tangible "here's what I promised you" moment. Say the word if you want me to build that mechanic (probably a simple `?early=<token>` query param on the Kickstarter link).
